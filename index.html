About

As a Ph.D. Candidate at the Gallogly College of Civil Engineering and Environmental Science at the University of Oklahoma, USA, I am advancing my expertise at the intersection of academic research and practical engineering applications. My academic foundations are rooted in Bangladesh University of Engineering & Technology (BUET), Dhaka, Bangladesh, where I earned both my bachelor’s and master’s degrees in Civil Engineering. Prior to embarking on my doctoral journey, I enriched my teaching portfolio as a Senior Lecturer in the Department of Civil Engineering at Daffodil International University and as a Lecturer at the University of Global Village, Bangladesh. Accumulating over seven years of professional experience, including four and a half years in academia and over three years (and counting) as a Graduate Research Assistant, I have cultivated a robust skill set that bridges theoretical knowledge with real-world practice.

Resume

Khondhaker Al Momin is currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Transportation Engineering while working as a graduate research assistant at the University of Oklahoma. He has previously worked as a senior lecturer at Daffodil International University. Holding interdisciplinary qualifications in civil engineering, system engineering, and data science, his research actively focuses on diverse areas, including traffic incident analysis and prevention, traffic flow theory, and ACES (autonomous, connected electric, and shared) vehicles. His interests also extend to big data analytics, network science, natural hazards, and system optimization.

Publications

I utilize various methods including supervised machine learning, social media data analysis, and traditional traffic analysis to understand complex issues in transportation. Specific areas of interest include pedestrian safety, travel mode choice, crisis response during compound disasters, and the impact of non-motorized vehicles on traffic flow.

The Institute of Transportation Engineers (ITE) is a professional association for transportation professionals who are responsible for meeting mobility and safety needs. Founded in 1930, ITE facilitates the application of technology and scientific principles to research, planning, functional design, implementation, operation, policy development, and management for any mode of transportation. The organization aims to enhance the flow of goods and services, improve the quality of life, and promote economic efficiency.

he American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) is a prominent professional body founded in 1852, making it the oldest national engineering society in the United States. ASCE represents more than 150,000 members of the civil engineering profession worldwide. Its mission is to provide essential value to its members and partners, advance civil engineering, and serve the public good. ASCE provides technical and professional conferences, primary research publications, a comprehensive continuing education program, and advocacy efforts for public policy. ASCE sets standards for the design and construction of infrastructure and buildings through the development of consensus-based standards that define the responsibilities of civil engineers.

The Institution of Engineers, Bangladesh (IEB) is the national professional organization for engineers in Bangladesh, established in 1948. Its primary role is to promote and advance the science, practice, and business of engineering in all its branches throughout Bangladesh. IEB serves as a central forum for engineers of various disciplines, offering a platform for professional networking, sharing knowledge, and advocating for technological and engineering advancements. IEB conducts a range of activities aimed at professional development, including organizing conferences, seminars, workshops, and training sessions. It also plays a crucial role in maintaining engineering standards by accrediting engineering degree programs and providing professional certification to engineers, ensuring that they meet the competency standards required in the industry.
